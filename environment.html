Kaieteur Falls

Kaieteur Falls is the largest single drop waterfall in the world. It is situated on the Potaro River in the Kaieteur National Park, in an area of the Amazon rainforest known as the Potaro-Siparuni region of Guyana. The drop over a sandstone and conglomerate cliff to the first break is 226 metres (741 feet) high. It then flows over a series of steep cascades, which, when measured, bring the total height to 251 metres (822 ft). While many falls have greater height, few have the combination of height and water volume, and Kaieteur is one of the world's most powerful waterfalls, with an average flow rate of 663 cubic meters per second (23,400 cubic feet per second).
